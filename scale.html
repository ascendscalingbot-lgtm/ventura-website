Step One
Know Your
Ideal Client

Before you post a single piece of content, you need to know exactly who you're talking to. Not "anyone who needs my service." One specific person. Their age. Their job. Their biggest problem at 2am. Their dream outcome.

Think of it like this: if you're shouting into a crowd, no one listens. But if you tap someone on the shoulder and say their name — they freeze and pay attention. Knowing your ICP is how you tap them on the shoulder.

📋 The 4 Things You Must Know About Your ICP
🎂
Who They Are
Age, job title, income level, family situation
😤
Their Problems
What keeps them up at night? What have they tried that didn't work?
🏆
Their Goals
What does their dream outcome look like in 90 days?
💬
Their Language
Exact words they use to describe their problem. Not yours.

Example ICP Profile — Fitness Coach:

Your action: Write down your ICP right now. Give them a name. Fill in every detail above. The more specific you are, the more powerfully your content will connect.

  • Write out a full ICP profile (name, age, job, income)
  • List their top 3 problems in their own words
  • Write out their dream outcome in one sentence
  • Identify 5 questions they would Google right now
  • Commit to this ICP — every piece of content is made for them
02
Step Two
Author the
Conversation

Your ideal client is already searching for answers. They're typing questions into Google, YouTube, and AI tools right now. Your job isn't to create content out of thin air — your job is to find those questions and answer them on camera.

When you answer the questions they're already asking, you don't interrupt their day — you show up exactly when they need you. That's the difference between content people skip and content people share.

🔍 How to Find Your ICP's Real Questions
🤖 Use AI to Do the Research

Copy and paste these prompts into Claude, ChatGPT, or Google:

What are the top 20 questions a [35-year-old busy dad] asks before hiring a [fitness coach]?
What does a [homeowner in Phoenix] search on Google before they call a [roofing company]?
Give me 15 YouTube video ideas for a [real estate agent] targeting [first-time homebuyers] in [Dallas, TX].
What fears and objections does a [small business owner] have before they hire a [marketing agency]?
🔎 Use Google's Own Free Tools
🔎
People Also Ask — Type your topic into Google and scroll down to the "People Also Ask" section. Those are real questions real people are typing right now. Screenshot them. Those are your next 10 videos.
🔗
Related Searches — Scroll to the very bottom of any Google results page. The related search suggestions hiding down there are gold mines for content ideas.
▶️
YouTube Autocomplete — Open YouTube and start typing your topic. Let it autocomplete and don't press enter yet. Every suggestion that pops up is something real people are searching for right now.
💬
Reddit — Search Reddit for your niche. Read how people describe their problems in their own exact words. Then use those exact words in your content — not your professional jargon.

Build a Content Bank: Aim for a list of 50 questions your ICP is asking. That's 50 videos. 50 posts. 50 chances to show up as the expert who has the answer.

🏆 Find Where Your ICP Actually Lives Online

Once you know exactly who your ICP is, take it to Claude or ChatGPT and ask: "Which social media platform does a [35-year-old male homeowner making $90K/year] spend the most time on?"

The answer might surprise you. A B2B consultant's ICP lives on LinkedIn. A fitness client is on Instagram and TikTok. A roofing customer is on Facebook and YouTube. Stop guessing. Let the data tell you.

Step Four
Shoot
Long-Form Video

Now that you know who you're talking to and what questions they're asking — it's time to answer those questions on camera. You don't need a studio. You don't need a $5,000 setup. You need an iPhone, decent light, and something real to say.

📱
Your Camera
An iPhone shot in landscape (horizontal/wide) mode is all you need. Shoot in 4K if your phone allows it. Modern iPhones shoot better than cameras that cost thousands a decade ago.
💡
Lighting
Face a window for natural light — free and looks incredible. Or grab a $30–$50 ring light from Amazon. Light in front of you, not behind you.
🎙️
Audio
Bad audio kills good content faster than bad video. A $20 lav mic that clips to your shirt is all you need. Shoot in a quiet room — no echo, no fans.
⏱️
Length
Aim for 7–25 minutes per video. Long enough to actually answer the question. Short enough to stay on point. If you go long — more clips to repurpose.
🎬 The Simple Video Formula
HOOK — Ask the question your ICP is already asking (0–30 sec)
CREDIBILITY — Why should they listen to you? (30 sec – 1 min)
CONTENT — Answer the question fully, teach everything (1–20 min)
PROOF — Share a result, case study, or example
CTA — Tell them what to do next (comment a word, grab the guide)
💬 ENGAGE — Reply to every comment in the first hour

Put your face on camera. This is non-negotiable. People buy from people they know, like, and trust. When your ICP watches 10 of your videos, they feel like they know you — and they haven't even spoken to you yet. No other medium builds trust this fast.

Now that you know your ICP's questions — take those same questions and use AI to build something they'd actually pay for. A guide. A toolkit. A checklist. A free plan. Then offer it for free in your videos.

This is where most people get it backwards: they hold back their best stuff and wonder why nobody trusts them. Give away your best ideas for free. The people who consume your free content become your best clients.

🧲 How to Build a Lead Magnet with AI

Use the 20–50 questions you've already researched from Step 2. These ARE the lead magnet.

Offer It In Your Videos
At the end of every video say: "Comment [KEYWORD] and I'll send you my free [guide name]." That's your automation trigger.
